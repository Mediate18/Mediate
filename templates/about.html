About this database

Welcome to the MEDIATE database. In this database, you will find data that has been extracted and enriched from a corpus of several hundred collections of private libraries sold (mostly at auction) in the Dutch Republic, France, British Isles and Italy between 1665 and 1830. In the main Sandbox dataset, this adds up to a total of 600 individual catalogues, which together list over half a million individual books.

This is a dynamic database, which is continually being updated and expanded. That means that records may contain errors, and data may change over time as it is further enriched and cleaned up. Our aim has not been to provide perfect transcriptions of every word in the collections, but to render the data extracted from the collections statistically manipulable. Since most collection transcriptions have been produced using OCR (optical character recognition) software, with some manual post-correction, a number of common OCR mistakes remain: long ‘s’ transcribed as ‘f’, ‘e’ transcribed as ‘c’, ‘a’ as ‘o’, ‘i’ as ‘t’, ‘l’ as ‘1’, etc.

How to cite this database

If referring to data from this database, please use the following citation form:

Alicia C. Montoya, Micha Hulsbosch, Helwi Blom, Evelien Chayes, Anna de Wilde, Rindert Jagersma, Juliette Reboul, and Joanna Rozendaal, MEDIATE database, 2022 – (ongoing), https://test.mediate-database.cls.ru.nl/ [date accessed].
