MEDIATE team members have manually classified book Items into one of the five subject categories used in the eightenth century by Parisian booksellers, the so-called 'Parisian booksellers' system' that was widely used by bibliographers, booksellers and cataloguers in France and beyond. This very rough classification system is intended primarily as a heuristic aid in understanding the contents of individual libraries or groups of libraries in the database. It can be used in conjunction with subject categorizations already present in some of the (French) catalogues, and will be supplemented later on by more fine-grained subject taxonomies.

For more on the Parisian booksellers' system, see H. Blom, 'Philosophie ou Commerce? L’évolution des systèmes de classement bibliographique dans les catalogues de bibliothèques privées publiés en France au XVIIIe siècle', in Frédéric Barbier, István Monok and Andrea Seidler (eds), Les bibliothèques et l’économie des connaissances/Bibliotheken und die Ökonomie des Wissens 1450–1850. Colloque international – Internationale Tagung, 9–13 avril/April 2019 Sárospatak (Hongrie/Ungarn), Budapest, Magyar Tudományos Akadémia Könyvtár és Információs Központ, 2020, pp. 203-234.
